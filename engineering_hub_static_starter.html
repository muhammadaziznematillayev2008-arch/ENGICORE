How to Become an Engineer

This is a practical roadmap from high school to professional engineering roles.

High school preparation

  • Focus on mathematics (calculus, algebra) and physics
  • Develop problem-solving and project skills
  • Join science clubs, robotics or programming teams

University degree paths

Most engineering careers start with a Bachelor's degree in an engineering discipline. Consider internships and hands-on projects during studies.

Skills & certifications

  • CAD, programming, lab skills, simulation tools
  • Certifications: FE (Fundamentals of Engineering) where applicable

Internships, projects, and competitions

Participate in internships, contribute to open-source or build projects. Competitions (e.g., robotics, coding contests) are highly valuable.

See Institutions for curated lists by region and specialization.
